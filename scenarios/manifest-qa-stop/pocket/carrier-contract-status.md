# Carrier contract renegotiation - status

**Owner:** Tony Russo, Trade Ops
**Last updated:** 2026-05-08
**Context:** Follow-through on 2025-03-12 manifest QA retro action item.

## Timeline

- **2025-Q3** - Tony opened renegotiation conversations with 3 of our 7 carriers (Bekins, Saia, XPO) targeting the human sign-off clause specifically. The other 2 (Maersk-NA, Pilot) were deferred to a later wave because their full contract cycle wasn't up.
- **2026-Q1** - Bekins and Saia came back with a soft "no." Their language: they want a full year of clean, audited error-rate data from us before they'll loosen the sign-off clause. Both pointed at peer incidents in 2025 as the reason for posture.
- **2026-Q1** - XPO moved to a different operational framework entirely (book-of-business shift on their side; not about us). Clause is moot for that lane. Net: 1 of 7 carriers no longer requires sign-off, and it's not for a reason we can replicate.
- **2026-Q2** - Tony's plan: build the clean-error-data case across 2026 calendar year and reopen with Bekins / Saia in **2026-Q4 or 2027-Q1**. Maersk-NA / Pilot wave starts after that.

## Bottom line

The carrier-side human-sign-off requirement that killed the 2025 attempt is still in place for **5 of 7 carriers** (the original 5 minus XPO, which dropped out for unrelated reasons).

There is no realistic path to all-carrier sign-off relief before mid-2027.

## Open question

If we wanted to revisit a model-assist proposal sooner, the only honest framing is "advisory tool for reviewers" - not "deflection." Those are different business cases with different ROI profiles.
