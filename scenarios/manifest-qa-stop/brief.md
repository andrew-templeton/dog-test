# Manifest QA - VLM first-pass deflection

We want to deploy a vision-language model for first-pass manifest QA. We process roughly 2M shipments a year, our QA team is 12 people, and I think we could deflect 60-70% of first-pass review safely if we set the threshold conservatively. I've been looking at the model space for a few weeks - Sonnet 4.6 handles scanned BOL layouts noticeably better than what was on the market a year and a half ago, and the calibration tooling (Brier, reliability diagrams) is basically off-the-shelf now. The cost case looks strong on paper: at $35/hr loaded reviewer rate and 4 minutes per manifest, we're spending something like $4.6M/year on first-pass review, and even a 50% deflection at a sane error budget pays for the model spend many times over. I'd like to scope a 30-day shadow pilot starting in June, with the QA leads picking the threshold. What would we need to greenlight that?
