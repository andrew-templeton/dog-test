# Inter-Rater Variance - Human Tagging

**Study date:** 2026-03-09 through 2026-03-20
**Sample:** 500 SKUs, randomly drawn from active catalog
**Raters:** All 4 ops associates (Mei Chen, Devon Brooks, Priya Anand, Luis Ortega)
**Design:** Each SKU tagged independently by 2 of 4 raters, balanced rotation

## Headline

- Pair-wise Cohen's kappa across 6 rater pairs: **0.34 average** (range 0.28 - 0.41)
- Reference: 0.4 - 0.6 = "moderate", below 0.4 = "fair". We are sitting in fair.

## Where the disagreements cluster

| Category boundary | Disagreement rate |
|---|---|
| athletic footwear vs running shoes vs sneakers | 41% of footwear SKUs |
| winter / cold-weather / insulated (hats, gloves) | 33% of cold-accessory SKUs |
| dress vs casual vs business-casual (men's tops) | 28% of menswear SKUs |
| activewear vs athleisure | 24% of women's tops |

## Worst-case observations

- 23 of 500 SKUs had **zero tag overlap** between the two human raters. Same product, no shared tags.
- Median tag-count per SKU varied by rater: Mei 5.1, Devon 3.6, Priya 4.8, Luis 3.9. Devon is systematically terser.
- Tag-count variance suggests no shared rubric on attribute granularity (1 tag vs 3 tags for the same attribute family).
