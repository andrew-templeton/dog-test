# Merch Ops Tagging Throughput

**Maintained by:** D. Park (merch ops lead)
**Last updated:** 2026-05-15

## Current state

- **Team:** 4 merch ops associates (T. Nguyen, R. Alvarez, S. Iyer, K. Brennan), fully loaded.
- **Throughput per associate:** ~80 SKUs/day on the tagging task (mixed with QA, attribute fixes, brand-team requests).
- **Working days per associate per year:** ~250 (after PTO, holidays, training).
- **Annual tagging capacity:** 4 × 80 × 250 = **80,000 SKU-tag operations/year**.

## Demand

- **Active catalog:** 50,000 SKUs.
- **Monthly turnover (new SKUs + meaningful attribute changes triggering retag):** **~12%**.
- **Annualized retag demand:** 50,000 × 12% × 12 = ~72K, plus net-new SKUs and exception-driven retags. Realistic load: **~75K tag operations/year**.

## Result

- Team is **at capacity**. Backlog has been growing roughly 200-400 SKUs/week through Q1 2026.
- Brand-side QA requests are routinely deferred 2-3 weeks behind the primary tagging queue.
- Two associates are flagged as flight risks in the most recent 1:1 notes (work-content complaint: "repetitive").

## Labor case for automation (independent of revenue case)

- Fully-loaded cost of the 4 associates: ~$280K/year.
- If LLM tagging absorbs the routine retag flow and leaves the team on exceptions, brand QA, and attribute curation: estimated **~$200K/year redeployable** (60-70% of routine load offloaded; rest remains human).
- This case stands whether or not the revenue lift materializes.
